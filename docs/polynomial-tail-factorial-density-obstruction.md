# Factorial-density obstruction for polynomial-tail equality

Consider the cleared integer orbit

\[
 Q_0=1,\qquad Q_1=d,\qquad
 Q_{n+2}=B(n+1)Q_n-A(n+2)Q_{n+1},                 \tag{1}
\]

where \(A(n)=pn+q\), \(B(n)=rn^2+un+v\), and put

\[
 E_N=\mathop{\rm lcm}_{0\leq n\leq N}
 \operatorname{denominator}\!\left(\frac{Q_n}{n!}\right). \tag{2}
\]

All denominators in (2) are in lowest terms and positive. This sharpens the
earlier finite-prime rate certificate to an exact finite-\(N\) factorial
divisor. No positivity or minimality assumption is used.

## 1. One surviving prime contributes a factorial part

Fix a prime \(\ell\). Suppose the reduced orbit never reaches the zero state:

\[
 (Q_n,Q_{n+1})\not\equiv(0,0)\pmod\ell
 \quad\hbox{for every }n\geq0.                         \tag{3}
\]

For every \(N\geq1\), at least one of \(Q_{N-1},Q_N\) is then an
\(\ell\)-adic unit. Since

\[
 v_\ell\!\left(
   \operatorname{denominator}\left(\frac{Q_n}{n!}\right)
 \right)
 =\max\{0,v_\ell(n!)-v_\ell(Q_n)\},                  \tag{4}
\]

the unit among those last two terms proves

\[
 \boxed{v_\ell(E_N)\geq v_\ell((N-1)!)}.             \tag{5}
\]

This is stronger than taking a recurrent nonzero residue somewhere in every
bounded block: the witness is always one of the final two indices.

Condition (3) has a one-line recurrence proof whenever every transfer step is
invertible. If \(B(n+1)\not\equiv0\pmod\ell\) and both \(Q_{n+1}\) and
\(Q_{n+2}\) vanished, (1) would give \(B(n+1)Q_n=0\), hence \(Q_n=0\).
Starting from \(Q_0=1\), induction rules out every zero pair. The algebraic
induction is formalized as `cleared_orbit_pair_ne_zero` in
`formal/PuckMathsFormal/PuckMathsFormal/PolynomialTail/IntegerOrbit.lean`.

More generally, (5) applies to every prime for which the particular state
survives, even if some transfer steps are singular. Reaching one zero pair is
equivalent to dying forever; otherwise no zero pair exists.

## 2. Nonsquare numerator discriminant

Let \(\Delta_B=u^2-4rv\) be a nonzero nonsquare integer. Exclude the finitely
many primes dividing \(2r\Delta_B\). If \(\ell\) is inert in
\(\mathbf Q(\sqrt{\Delta_B})\), then \(B\) has no root over
\(\mathbf F_\ell\). Every transfer step is invertible and (5) holds,
independently of the initial increment \(d\). Consequently, for every
\(N\geq1\),

\[
 \boxed{
   \prod_{\substack{\ell\ \mathrm{inert}\ \mathrm{in}\\
                 \mathbf Q(\sqrt{\Delta_B})}}
       \ell^{v_\ell((N-1)!)}\ \mid\ E_N .}            \tag{6}
\]

Only primes \(\ell\leq N-1\) contribute, so the product is finite. On the
other hand every denominator in (2) divides \(N!\), and therefore

\[
 E_N\mid N!.                                           \tag{7}
\]

The prime number theorem for the nonprincipal quadratic character of
\(\mathbf Q(\sqrt{\Delta_B})\), together with Legendre's formula, gives

\[
 \sum_{\ell\ \mathrm{inert}}
   v_\ell((N-1)!)\log\ell
   =\frac12N\log N+O(N).                               \tag{8}
\]

One way to see the constant is to split the prime-factorization identity for
\(\log((N-1)!)\) by the two values of the quadratic character. The
character-weighted difference is \(O(N)\); the finitely many ramified primes
also contribute only \(O(N)\). Equations (6)--(8) prove

\[
 \boxed{
 \frac12\leq
   \liminf_{N\to\infty}\frac{\log E_N}{N\log N}
 \leq
   \limsup_{N\to\infty}\frac{\log E_N}{N\log N}
 \leq1.}                                               \tag{9}
\]

Thus the nonsquare-numerator branch does not merely have superexponential
common denominators: at least one half of the logarithmic mass of a factorial
is forced into them. This holds for every rational initial line, positive or
not.

## 3. The state-specific bridge in the exceptional branch

Let \(S_d\) be the set of primes for which the state generated by \((1,d)\)
never dies modulo \(\ell\). Applying (5) to an arbitrary finite subset
\(S\subseteq S_d\), then letting \(N\) tend to infinity, gives

\[
 \liminf_{N\to\infty}\frac{\log E_N}{N}
 \geq \sum_{\ell\in S}\frac{\log\ell}{\ell-1}.        \tag{10}
\]

It follows that exponential factorial reduction \(E_N\leq C^N\) is possible
only if

\[
 \boxed{
   \sum_{\ell\in S_d}\frac{\log\ell}{\ell-1}<\infty.} \tag{11}
\]

Now assume the unresolved exceptional conditions

\[
 \Delta_B\in\mathbf Q^2,\qquad
 \Delta_c=p^2+4r\notin\mathbf Q^2,\qquad
 R=p(u-r)-2rq\ne0.                                    \tag{12}
\]

At every unramified prime inert for \(\Delta_c\), the period monodromy has
determinant zero and nonzero trace. Hence it has rank one, and the particular
orbit dies exactly when

\[
 M_\ell(1,d)^t=0.                                     \tag{13}
\]

This kernel condition also has an exact differential-equation form. For
\(0\leq n<\ell\), \(n!\) is invertible in \(\mathbf F_\ell\), so define

\[
 F_\ell(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\ell-1}\frac{Q_n}{n!}z^n.
\]

The coefficient identity for the EGF equation shows

\[
 M_\ell(1,d)^t=0
 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
 \begin{cases}
 \deg F_\ell<\ell,\\
 F_\ell(0)=1,\quad F_\ell'(0)=d,\\
 (1+pz-rz^2)F_\ell''
   =((3r+u)z-(2p+q))F_\ell'+(r+u+v)F_\ell.
 \end{cases}                                         \tag{14}
\]

Indeed, the ordinary recurrence verifies all coefficients below the top two;
the coefficients at degrees \(\ell-2\) and \(\ell-1\) are precisely the
boundary conditions \(Q_\ell=Q_{\ell+1}=0\). Conversely those two zero terms
make the truncated EGF an exact polynomial solution, since both sides of the
differential equation have degree at most \(\ell-1\).

The inert primes in (12) have divergent Mertens weight. Combining
(11)--(14) shows that any exponentially factorial-reduced exceptional state
must satisfy (13), equivalently (14), at **all but a Mertens-negligible set**
of those inert
primes: the exceptional nonkernel primes must have convergent
\(\sum\log\ell/(\ell-1)\).

This is the exact state-specific target for a p-adic classification of
factorial reduction. A proof that a fixed integer seed cannot lie in these
varying Hensel/kernel lines at almost every inert prime, unless the recurrence
is on a known rational or aligned locus, would imply that classification. No
such uniform nonvanishing theorem is claimed here. Equation (14) identifies
it as a fixed-initial-jet polynomial-solution, or Hasse/p-curvature-kernel,
condition.

Crucially, even that theorem would **not** finish integer equality. It would
prove that an exceptional positive minimal orbit, if one exists, is not
factorially reduced; factorial-density growth is compatible with positivity.
The equality problem still needs an independent implication from positivity
to factorial reduction, or a direct archimedean/sign contradiction. On the
irrational-exponent stratum, positivity-to-factorial-reduction is essentially
as strong as the desired nonexistence theorem rather than a consequence of
the modular estimates above.

## 4. Formalization boundary

The Lean file now contains the recurrence lemma that nonvanishing quadratic
coefficients preserve a nonzero adjacent state. It has deliberately not been
built while the unrelated repository build break is being handled elsewhere.
The following ingredients are not yet kernel-checked:

1. the valuation identity (4) and simultaneous divisor statement (6);
2. the quadratic-character prime-distribution estimate (8);
3. the uniform cyclic-continuant trace formula used to pass from (12) to the
   rank-one description in (13);
4. the truncated-EGF equivalence (14), although its coefficient proof is
   already contained algebraically in `GeneratingFunction.lean`.

The first is elementary `Nat.factorization` bookkeeping. The second requires
analytic number theory not currently developed in the project. The third
already has a written proof and exhaustive exact regressions, but remains the
known finite-field Lean gap.
