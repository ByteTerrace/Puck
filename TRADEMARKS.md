## Plain-language summary (not the legal terms)

**The code is source-available. The names are not.** You may use ByteTerrace.Puck's source under

Puck's source license (**PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0**, plus any commercial license) grants

## 3. Uses you may make **without asking** (nominative / fair use)

You do not need permission to make **truthful, non-confusing references** to Puck or ByteTerrace:

- Stating, accurately, that your product **is built on, uses, is compatible with, or interoperates
  with** ByteTerrace.Puck — e.g. "Powered by Puck," "a game built on ByteTerrace Puck." Use the
  Mark only as much as needed to make the true statement; don't make it the most prominent element.
- **Discussing, teaching, reviewing, reporting on, or comparing** Puck or ByteTerrace.
- **Redistributing unmodified copies** of the official software under its license, keeping the name
  "Puck" as we shipped it.
- Naming files, packages, or variables where the name is **descriptive and technically necessary**
  (e.g. importing a package we named).

When you refer to the Marks, please: use them **as adjectives, not nouns or verbs** ("the Puck
engine," not "a Puck" / "to Puck"); don't alter, abbreviate, or combine them into a new name; and
don't imply sponsorship or endorsement you don't have.

## 5. Modified and forked versions

You are free (per the code license) to fork and modify Puck. To keep users from being misled, a
modified or forked distribution **must**: (a) use a **distinct name** that is not "Puck" and not
confusingly similar; (b) **not** use the Logos; and (c) make clear it is **not** the official
ByteTerrace.Puck and is not endorsed by ByteTerrace. Truthful attribution ("based on Puck") is
encouraged.

and consistency. ByteTerrace reserves the right to review use of the Marks and to ask that any use
be modified or stopped. As **registered** marks, they carry a legal presumption of ByteTerrace's
ownership and of its exclusive right to use them for the registered goods and services. **Failure
